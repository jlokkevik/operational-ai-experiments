# Ideas & Learnings
## AI-assisted development changes early-stage product work

One of the more interesting observations so far is how AI-assisted development changes early-stage product work and communication between business and development.

Modern AI tooling makes it significantly easier to rapidly prototype ideas, test workflows and create clickable mockups or lightweight proof-of-concepts before investing heavily in implementation.

This improves the ability to:

* Clarify scope early
* Explore workflows iteratively
* Validate assumptions faster
* Communicate product ideas more clearly with developers and stakeholders

Rather than replacing traditional software engineering, these workflows seem to improve collaboration and reduce friction in the early phases of product and solution development.

---

## Persistent AI workflows are more operationally complex than expected

Running AI-assisted workflows continuously on VPS infrastructure quickly introduces operational considerations:

* Reliability
* Security
* Context handling
* Workflow persistence
* Error handling
* Long-running processes

Even relatively small experiments become closer to operating systems than simply "using AI".

---

## Context and memory seem more important than raw intelligence

One recurring observation is that workflow quality often depends more on:

* Context structure
* Information retrieval
* Persistent memory
* Tool integrations

than on the raw capability of the underlying model itself.

---

## AI lowers the barrier to experimentation

AI-assisted development makes it significantly easier to test ideas that previously would have required much deeper technical specialization.

For me personally, this has made it possible to explore:

* Product ideas
* Automation workflows
* Self-hosted systems
* Agent-based interactions

in a much more hands-on and iterative way than before.
